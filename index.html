Top Spenders

Highlighting the top economic spenders

  • United States of America

    Highest Absolute Spender

    The United States is the highest spender in the world in terms of absolute USD spent on Education, Military and Healthcare. When viewed in terms of Population or GDP, it is a consistent high spender, but not always top.

  • Australia

    Education per Capita

    Australia has the highest education budget per capita in the world, and has held that title from 2011-2017.

  • Saudi Arabia

    Military Spender

    Saudi Arabia is the highest spender in the world in terms of both military expense per capita and per GDP

  • Timor-Leste

    Highest Percent Growth - Healthcare

    Timor-Leste had the highest percent growth in healthcare expenditure from 2011-2017. Timor-Leste is a small island nation in Oceania, just north of Australia.

  • Laos

    Highest Percent Growth - Education

    The Lao People's Democratic Republic had the highest percent growth in education expenditure from 2011-2017. Laos is located in southeast Asia, east of Thailand.

  • Mali

    Highest Percent Growth - Military

    Mali had the highest percent growth in military expenditure from 2011-2017. Mali is located in West Africa, and is the 8th largest country in Africa.

Sources and Methods

Data Sources and Analysis Methods

Data Sources

Healthcare Data: Current Health Expenditure (% of GDP) - WorldBank

Military Data: Military Expenditure (current USD) - WorldBank

GDP Data: GDP (current USD) - WorldBank

Population Data: Population, total - WorldBank

Education Data: Government Expenditure on Education, total (% of GDP) - WorldBank

Supplimental Educational Data: Public spending on education Primary to tertiary, % of GDP, 1995 – 2016 - OECD

Data Analysis

Data from all datasets were cleaned and aggregated in Python 3.6.4 using the included script. Only data from the past 10 years were considered. The past 10 years were sampled, and 2011-2017 were selected for analysis, as this span was the most complete across the metrics of interest.

Some datasets (notably Healthcare and Education) were originally sourced as % GDP, rather than current USD. These datasets were converted to current USD using the GDP data for that country.

Where datapoints were missing, but at least 2 datapoints existed for the 5 years of analysis, a linear model was fit to the existing data. The predicted datapoints were interpolated into the dataset. If less than two datapoints existed, the country was removed from the analysis.

Per Capita Healthcare vs Per Capita GDP

Compare the GDP per person and Healthcare per person

Position on the y axis represents per Capita GDP, bubble size shows the per Capita Healthcare spending. Position on the x-axis shows fiscal year.

Observations

The United States has only recently acheived the highest GDP per capita of the top 10 healthcare spenders, but spends significantly more on healthcare per capita than all other countries.

Both China and Brazil spend a suprisingly small amount on healthcare per capita, and also produce a relatively small amount of GDP per capita. However, both countries have large populations, so while the per capita rates are low, the total values are high.
